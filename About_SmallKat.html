The SmallKat Project is an ongoing project, starting in October 2017 in which we undertook a project were between myself and one other student, we designerd, built and programmed a walking quadruped using low cost servo motors in 7 weeks. At the end of the term, we had successfully completed the project and moved onto our other courses. The project then came back to live as a precursor to or MQP(Senior project) in which we developed V2. Two of these cats wer printed, one red, one grey commically called crimson and grey

The development of Crimson and grey came with better servos, a much more capable motherboard, power regulation and pressure sensors imbedded within the feet. With all of these developments came a far superior walking gait making the project overall far more impressive.

The motherbaord developed allowed us to regulate the battery voltage to the motors and have some form of feed back to the system it integrated 4 quad channel current sense ICs and 2 sensor fused IMUs. This all fit within the form factor of the Raspberry pi 3B+ and allowed for pin to pin connection for USB HID communication. The ability to perform current sensing on the motors allowed us to inversely calculate the force vector on the end effector. The IMUs allowed us to quickly and accurately attain our roll, pitch and yaw ar 2 extremities of the robot and then adjust our gait accordingly.
